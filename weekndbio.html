OUT OF TIME

Early Life

Born in Toronto in 1990 to Ethiopian immigrants, Tesfaye was raised by his mother and grandmother in Scarborough. He is fluent in Amharic and French. He dropped out of school at seventeen, adopting his stage name "The Weeknd" after leaving home one weekend (removing the 'e' to avoid trademark issues). His early experiences with homelessness, incarceration, and drug use significantly influenced his music.

Breakthrough

Tesfaye began releasing music anonymously on YouTube in 2009, collaborating with producer Jeremy Rose. His identity was initially undisclosed, gaining wider attention after being acknowledged by Drake. In 2011, he co-founded the XO record label and released three mixtapes—House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence—which established his distinctive alternative R&B sound.

Evolution

He signed with Republic Records, reissuing his early mixtapes as Trilogy in 2012, followed by his debut studio album, Kiss Land, in 2013. His career then shifted towards a more pop-oriented sound with albums like Beauty Behind the Madness (2015) and Starboy (2016), both of which reached number one on the US Billboard 200 and produced multiple chart-topping singles. He returned to alternative R&B with My Dear Melancholy (2018) and later explored dream pop and new wave with After Hours (2020), which featured the record-breaking single "Blinding Lights."

Influence

His recent projects include Dawn FM (2022) and the upcoming Hurry Up Tomorrow (2025). He also made a controversial acting debut in The Idol (2023). The Weeknd has achieved significant commercial success, selling over 75 million records, earning numerous diamond certifications, and setting records for Spotify streams, establishing him as one of the world's best-selling artists and an influential figure in music.
